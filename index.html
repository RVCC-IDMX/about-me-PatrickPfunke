Image hover effects in CSS

Image hover effects are effects used to show animation in a website's visuals, they make the site more interesting and interactive allowing the viewer to have more fun and spend more time on the site.

Hover effects can take many forms, such as buttons and links but the most creative form is image hovers, while hovering over an image you can make it take many forms, Image hover effects are a way to spice up your images and make your website feel more fun and hi-tech. Some simple hover effects could include Grayscale to Color, fade in images, growing animations, shrinking animations, square images to circle images, a 3D shadow effect, adding a border around our images, blurring the image, we can even change the orientation of the image by rotating it, we can even brighten up an image upon hover.

All these effects can be done with some simple coding and can have a large impact on the outlook of your website. Adding hover effects into your website makes it easier for the reader to know where to click. There are many places to find these animations pre-made, probably locked behind a paywall to access them, but it can be a useful tool for those needing animations. Hover effects have a high payoff when implemented with a low cost of effort, which is mainly the reason behind them being one of the most used effects on websites.

If you want to, try going to a popular website or one of your choosing and look out for hover effects, Im sure it won't take long to identify one if not multiple. Another example of this is in news articles that often advertise subscriptions, for example when you access a site and try to leave there is a pop-up that tries to get you to subscribe to their service. Interactions between the user and the website is a very important part of any site, keeping users engaged and encouraging them to spend more time exploring and pressing buttons.

The image hover effect I wanted to focus on was a way to display the text while hovering over a photo, since in this project we have descriptions under each photo, instead of a simple zoom-in or tilt, so the method I used was to shift the figure caption up by -100%, this moves the figure captions out of the hidden view and into view. If you were to mess with the translation percentage and set it to a higher percentage, it is possible that the figure caption could be displayed prior to hovering over the image.

Another form of hover effect that I was attempting to use was to hover over and darken the image and display text, but this required the use of divs, but this doesn't work well with the way I chose to display the gallery, and would often break the structure of the gallery section, so I had to abort that idea and move onto another version that could be easily implemented into the structure of my code. An example of this is shown above in the gallery section, displaying how after hovering over each image the text is shown.
